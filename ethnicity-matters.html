Why Ethnicity Matters

We need donors from every community. Why? Because blood and organ compatibility is influenced by genetic background, which varies between ethnic groups. When donors reflect the diversity of patients, it increases the chances of successful, life-saving transplants.

Did you know?

Black, Asian and minority-ethnic patients often wait significantly longer for a successful match than white patients, due to a shortage of suitably matched donors.

If more people with these ethnic backgrounds donated their organs after death, or as living donors, transplant waiting times would fall dramatically.

1. Better Compatibility

Blood types and tissue markers are more likely to match between people of the same ethnicity. For bone marrow and kidney donations especially, genetic markers called HLA (Human Leukocyte Antigens) are crucial and inherited.

2. Reduce Waiting Time

Patients from minority ethnic groups often wait longer for a suitable donor because there are fewer registered donors from their communities. Increasing donor diversity helps bridge this gap.

3. Better Transplant Outcomes

Closer genetic matches lower the risk of rejection and lead to improved long-term success, fewer complications, and better quality of life for recipients.

4. Promoting Fairness in Healthcare

Everyone deserves an equal chance at life-saving treatment. By registering as a donor, you help make healthcare fairer and more accessible for all, regardless of race or heritage.
