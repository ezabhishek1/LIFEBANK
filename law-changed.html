The Law Has Changed

On 20 May 2020, England introduced a new "opt out" system for organ donation. This means that all adults will be considered to have agreed to donate their organs when they die, unless they have recorded a decision not to donate or are in one of the excluded groups.

Who does it affect?

This law applies to people who:

  • Are over 18 years old
  • Live in England
  • Have lived in England for more than 12 months
  • Have the mental capacity to understand the change

It does not apply to children, short-term residents, or people who lack capacity.

Why was this law introduced?

The change aims to save more lives by increasing the number of organs available for transplant. Surveys show most people support organ donation but never get around to registering.

The opt-out system makes it easier to honor people's intention to donate.

What do you need to do?

It is still your choice whether or not to become an organ donor.

  • You can register your decision to donate.
  • You can opt out if you do not want to donate.
  • Share your decision with your family so they are aware of your wishes.
